1) Who we are

UbuntuFX is an education-focused brand providing trading-related learning resources, newsletters, market commentary, templates, and digital products. This Privacy Policy applies to our website and related services that link to it.

Quick note: We do not ask for sensitive personal information as a condition to access free educational content. If you ever see a request that looks suspicious, contact us using the details in the Contact section.

2) Information we collect

We collect information in three main ways: (a) information you provide, (b) information collected automatically, and (c) information from service providers.

A. Information you provide

  • Contact details: such as your email address when you subscribe to our newsletter or contact us.
  • Messages: information you include when you email us or submit a form (for example, questions or feedback).
  • Optional details: any additional information you choose to share, like your name or trading interests.

B. Information collected automatically

  • Device & usage data: IP address, browser type, device identifiers, pages viewed, and timestamps.
  • Approximate location: derived from your IP address (city/region level, not precise GPS).
  • Cookies & similar tech: to remember preferences and help us understand site usage.

C. Information from service providers

If we use tools for forms, email delivery, analytics, or website hosting, those providers may process data on our behalf. We only use providers we believe are reputable and necessary for delivering the service.

3) How we use information

We use your information to operate UbuntuFX and provide a better experience. Typical uses include:

  • Delivering newsletters, updates, and educational content.
  • Responding to questions, support requests, or feedback.
  • Improving site performance, design, and content quality.
  • Protecting against fraud, spam, and abuse.
  • Complying with legal obligations when applicable.
Risk-first principle: We aim to collect only what we need. If we don’t need it to run the service, we avoid collecting it.

4) When we share information

We do not sell your personal information. We may share information only in limited cases:

A. Service providers

We may share data with vendors who help us run the site or deliver services (e.g., hosting, email delivery, analytics). They are permitted to process data only to provide services to us.

B. Legal and safety

We may disclose information if required by law, to respond to lawful requests, or to protect the rights, safety, and security of UbuntuFX, our users, or the public.

C. Business changes

If UbuntuFX is involved in a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction. If this happens, we will take reasonable steps to notify users where required.

5) Cookies & analytics

Cookies are small files stored on your device. We may use cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Keep the site working properly (security and core features).
  • Remember preferences (like theme or consent choices, where implemented).
  • Understand which pages are useful, so we can improve content.

You can control cookies through your browser settings. If you disable certain cookies, some parts of the site may not work as intended.

If you want to opt out of analytics tracking (where applicable), you can also use browser add-ons or privacy tools that block trackers.

6) Email & newsletters

If you subscribe, we send a weekly email briefing and occasional important updates. You can unsubscribe at any time by using the unsubscribe link in our emails, or by contacting us.

  • What we send: educational insights, market context, key levels, risk reminders, and product/resource updates.
  • What we avoid: spam, selling your email, or sending unrelated marketing blasts.

Please note: email delivery is handled by service providers and may include basic delivery metrics (e.g., whether an email was delivered or opened) depending on the provider settings.

7) Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards to protect your information. However, no internet-based service can be 100% secure. If you believe your interaction with us is no longer secure, contact us immediately.

  • Access controls and limited permissions for internal tools.
  • Monitoring and spam protection on forms where available.
  • Best-effort data minimization and retention practices.

8) Data retention

We retain personal information only as long as necessary for the purposes described in this policy, including legal, accounting, or reporting requirements.

  • Newsletter subscription data is kept until you unsubscribe or request deletion.
  • Support messages may be retained to improve support quality and maintain records of issues.
  • Basic logs may be retained for security and performance analysis.

9) Your rights & choices

Depending on your location, you may have rights regarding your personal information. These may include:

  • Access: request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Correction: request updates to inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Deletion: request deletion of your information (where legally allowed).
  • Objection / restriction: object to certain processing or request limitations.
  • Unsubscribe: opt out of marketing emails anytime.

To exercise these rights, contact us using the details in the Contact section. We may need to verify your request to protect your privacy.

10) Children’s privacy

UbuntuFX is intended for a general audience and is not designed to knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information to us, please contact us and we will take appropriate steps to address it.

11) International transfers

If you access UbuntuFX from outside the country where our service providers are located, your information may be processed in other countries. We take reasonable steps to ensure appropriate safeguards are in place when using vendors and hosting providers.

12) Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the “Last updated” date above. If changes are significant, we may provide additional notice where appropriate.
