1) What Is a Macro Regime?

Macro regimes are prevailing economic environments shaping capital flows. They combine growth, inflation, policy, and liquidity phases. Understanding the regime helps align trading strategies with systemic flows:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}.

2) Growth Cycles and Capital Rotation

Expansionary periods attract capital to strong-growth economies. Contraction leads to retreat into safe-haven currencies. Mapping growth momentum across regions provides directional clarity:contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.

3) Inflation Regimes and Policy Response
