Glossary

Terms we use (simple definitions)

If you’re new, don’t stress the jargon. Use these definitions to follow the outlook step-by-step.

Structure & Market Behavior
Market Structure
The pattern of highs/lows that shows trend or range. Helps you trade with direction, not emotion.
BOS (Break of Structure)
A clear break of a prior swing that supports continuation (trend strength).
CHOCH (Change of Character)
A shift in behavior that suggests a possible reversal or transition from trend to range (or vice versa).
Liquidity
Areas where orders are likely sitting (often around obvious highs/lows). Price may “grab” these areas.
Levels & Planning
Key Level
A price zone that matters (support/resistance, prior high/low, equilibrium). Used for reactions and invalidation.
Scenario
A “if this happens, then we do this” plan. We prepare multiple outcomes.
Invalidation
The point where your idea is proven wrong. It protects you from hope-trading.
Risk Management
How you size and protect capital (position size, stop placement, max daily loss). Non-negotiable.
